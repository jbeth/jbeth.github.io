Lesson two covered the use and customization of a few popular web browsers, including Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Opera, Brave, and Safari. The third activity in this lesson had us review and configure various features in these browsers after installation. My biggest takeaway from this was just how similar all of these browsers are when it comes to setting configurations. Even if the menus are located in separate places or designed in slightly different layouts, the core locations of most settings are relatively similar. Knowing this, it should be far easier for me to work in unfamiliar environments such as macOS concerning troubleshooting and support as my company expands our environment to include Apple platforms.

The next activity in this lesson that I will be reflecting on is activity five, which had us view, install, and disable addons in our browsers. In this lesson, I discovered that addons I had been using for years that may have once been supported only in Chrome or Firefox were now available cross-platform in other browsers. This has made me re-evaluate why I use Chrome as my preferred browser and encouraged me to investigate other browsers to see if they have comparable features and use cases to supplement my work in ways that Chrome may not be designed or equipped to do.
